🎵 Dissecting a Rhythm Game Masterpiece: "folern"
— How Nuyuri Creates Immersion Beyond Words.

📅 2026.04.20 ✍️ Project SEKAI Analysis Lab 🏷️ #ProjectSEKAI #folern #CHUNITHM #MusicAnalysis

Have you heard of "folern"? Added to Project SEKAI in January 2024, this track stunned rhythm game players with its overwhelming density and stylish composition.

But the true genius of this song lies in its profound ambiguity — it feels like you understand it, yet you can't quite grasp it. Today, let's dive deep into why "folern" captivates so many players.

🎧 An Anomaly in the World of Project SEKAI

In Project SEKAI, you can unlock this song for 10 music cards. There is no Virtual Singer version, nor a 3DMV or 2DMV. And yet, it possesses a gravitational pull that makes players call it a "masterpiece."

⚡ First play shock — the blistering BPM of 200, the relentless phrases, and a "voice" that you can't quite decipher. That sense of alienness, so different from familiar Vocaloid tracks, is the first impression "folern" leaves.

🗣️ The Mystery That Draws You In: Unintelligible Lyrics

The most unique aspect of this song is its lyrics. In fact, there are no actual lyrics. The vocal-like sounds that emerge midway are sung in a fictional language.

"Happy to be, turn to refrain…"
— These aren't meaningful words. Yet they etch themselves into your brain as a "song."

The phrase "Happy to be, turn to refrain" sounds plausible, but many players speculate it carries no semantic meaning.

This "absence of meaning" is precisely what ignites our imagination. Unbound by concrete messages, we are compelled to *feel* something within the music. It's akin to standing before a vast landscape or an abstract painting. The sound itself shakes our emotions beyond language.

📖 The Hidden Mythology Behind the Art

Another essential piece of the puzzle is the epic backstory hidden within the jacket illustration.

The figure depicted is "Sylvius," a "musician" of divine descent. He stands in opposition to the goddess "Astraea," who appears on the jacket of another CHUNITHM track, "Endmark ni Kibou to Namida wo Soete."

This means "folern" isn't just a standalone track — it's a single scene in a larger narrative that transcends the rhythm game itself. The tension and pathos in the music might very well be an expression of their conflict.

⚙️ Achieving the Ultimate "Flow State" Through Chart Design

The MASTER chart (Lv.34) in Project SEKAI is a superhuman challenge: blistering speed, one-hand patterns, staircase streams, and intricate trills. Yet, strangely, it never feels "impossible." The exquisite placement of notes promises catharsis upon completion.

This is where the "optimal difficulty" is engineered, and before you know it, you've hit the retry button again.

✨ Will You Fall for "folern"?

What did you think? "folern" is beloved and hailed as a masterpiece not just for its cool sound, but for its layered allure: the mystery of its lyrics, the weight of its mythos, and its perfection as a rhythm game track.

If you haven't played it yet, I urge you to dive in. What will you feel when you listen to this track?

🦖 Turning Weakness into Power — The Modern Cry of "Kaijuu ni Naritai" (I Want to Be a Monster).

📅 2026.04.20 ✍️ Project SEKAI Analysis Lab 🏷️ #ProjectSEKAI #KaijuuNiNaritai #Sakuzyo #APPEND #Analysis

Among the tracks in Project SEKAI, "Kaijuu ni Naritai" stands out as particularly striking. Composed by Sakuzyo (a.k.a. "Sakujo"), this song hammers home a painful message at a blistering BPM of 220.

The title itself is intense. "I Want to Be a Monster" — isn't this the very subconscious cry of modern individuals wanting to destroy their weaker selves and leave a mark on the world?

📋 Track Data: "Kaijuu ni Naritai"

  • Composer / ArrangerSakuzyo
  • BPM220
  • Durationapprox. 2:18
  • Added to PJSKAugust 2023 (Music Shop)
  • GenreOther
  • Highest DifficultyAPPEND Lv.38 (as of April 2026)

💥 The Space Between Life and Death in the Lyrics

The narrator of this song is clearly a being on the brink of death. Cast out by society, ignored by everyone. In the depths of despair, their final wish is a destructive impulse: "I want to be a monster."

"It's wrong, it's wrong, I don't want to live in a world like this, but..."
"It's wrong, it's wrong, yet still, I wish to remain myself."

It's a contradiction. Not wanting to live, yet wishing to "remain myself." This ambivalent emotion is the core of the song. The desire to be acknowledged, to be loved — and when that fails, the desire to at least become something to be feared. It's the ultimate flip side of a desperate need for self-affirmation.

🎬 The Catharsis of Destruction in the 2DMV

The original 2DMV in Project SEKAI features abstract animation. The "monster" that appears destroys a city and roars at something. But am I the only one who sees a certain sadness in its eyes?

What makes this MV brilliant is that it releases the "destruction" we cannot enact in real life, within the dreamscape of the video. For a few minutes, we can project our daily frustrations onto the monster and let them explode.

⌨️ Becoming the Monster Through the APPEND Chart (Lv.38)

And for rhythm game players, the APPEND chart is essential. At Lv.38 — the current highest difficulty (as of April 2026) — it *forces* the player to "become a monster."

⚡ High-speed trills, cross-hand patterns, rapid chord streams... your fingers tangle and your stamina drains. Yet, the moment you demolish the chart, you feel a sense of unity with the monster on the screen. This is the true experience of "Kaijuu ni Naritai."

The chart designer didn't just create a hard map. They designed a configuration that pushes human limits precisely to give players the immersion of being a monster destroying notes.

🧠 Why Do We Long to Be Monsters?

From a psychological perspective, the desire to "become a monster" can be seen as a projection of the "Shadow" archetype. In Jungian psychology, humans repress socially unacceptable aggression and destructive impulses into the unconscious as the "Shadow." This shadow often manifests in imagery of beasts and monsters.

Perhaps listening to this song allows us to temporarily confront and release our own shadows. That's why a strange sense of exhilaration lingers after the song ends.

✨ It Starts With Acknowledging "Weakness"

"Kaijuu ni Naritai" is by no means a song that glorifies destruction. Rather, it's a painfully sincere song of self-affirmation, one that stares directly at personal weakness and societal discomfort, yet still screams "I want to be me."

We can't actually become monsters. But by listening to this song, by playing this chart, we can dialogue with the little monster inside our hearts. And maybe, just maybe, we can find the strength to carry that tiny scream with us into tomorrow.
