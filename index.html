Pro Tips: How to Actually Clear This (Eventually)

After 30+ failed attempts and finally scraping a clear, here's the survival kit you need. Forget your usual settings—this chart demands adaptation.

1. Speed Settings (Critical): You might think slowing down helps. It doesn't. Crank your note speed to 10.8 – 11.2. At lower speeds, the notes clump together and become an unreadable mess. Higher speed spreads them out, giving your brain a split-second longer to process the trill patterns. Trust me on this—going from 10.5 to 11.0 was the single biggest factor in me clearing the chorus.

2. The 0:45 Trap (Right Hand Stamina Check): The chorus starts with a brutal right-hand trill combined with left-hand flicks. The natural instinct is to tense up both hands. Resist that. Consciously relax your left hand. It only needs to hit every other beat. Your right hand is doing the heavy lifting. If your left hand is tense, you'll over-flick and break your combo. Focus all your energy on keeping that right-hand trill smooth.

3. Finger Placement (Thumbs vs. Fingers): If you're a thumb player, I salute your courage, but you're playing on hard mode. This chart was designed for multi-finger play. Use index and middle fingers on both hands (4-finger style). Thumbs simply cannot sustain the 14.7 taps/second for two minutes straight without cramping. If you must use thumbs, stretch them frequently between attempts.

4. Mental Reset (The "Flow State" Hack): Don't look at your combo counter. Don't look at the "GREAT" judgments. Stare at the exact center of the judgment line. Let your peripheral vision catch the notes. This forces your brain to switch from "conscious parsing" (which is too slow) to "pattern chunking" (which is automatic). It sounds like woo-woo meditation, but it's the only way to survive the 1:20-1:50 climax.
