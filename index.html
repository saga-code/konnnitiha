Most high-level charts have "breathing room"—a few seconds where the note density drops so you can reset your brain. Kaijuu doesn't. Once the chorus hits, you're locked in a dense stream of 16th notes for nearly two minutes straight. If you miss one note, there's no chance to re-sync. You just drown.

Who is Sakuzyo (削除)?

Born in 1994, Sakuzyo is a legend in the rhythm game world, known for brutal yet beautiful tracks like "Altale" and "Destr0yer"[reference:5]. His specialty isn't just speed—it's removing your escape routes. With "Kaijuu ni Naritai," he distilled his entire philosophy into one Project SEKAI chart: no tempo changes, no breaks, just pure, unrelenting density.

He described the track as a challenge to players: "Become a monster and destroy the falling notes."[reference:6]
